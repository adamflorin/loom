Loom makes generative music
in Ableton Live.

* Live 9 + Max For Live required

To install, open Loom.alp and install it wherever you like.

I like to put it next to the User Library and then Add Folder... in Live's Browser to point to it.

Concepts

Loom generates MIDI patterns one gesture at a time.

A gesture is a rhythmic grouping of notes.

Every instance of the Loom plugin is a player.

Players may hand off control amongst one another. (See Phrasing below.)

A generator is a random number generator:

The bars represent a normal distribution of random numbers from low to high.

The mean shifts the probability from low to high (meaning based on context).

The deviation expands the probability that the generated number will fall further from the mean.

Controls




Broadly, Loom has two types of controls: phrasing (left), and gestural (right).

Phrasing

Phrasing determines when a player starts and stops, relative to other players.

  • if on play is checked, play when Live transport starts
  • play immediately when now is pressed
  • play after another player finishes playing or with another player (starting at the same time) if box is checked and player is selected from the below menu
  • continue playing forever (∞) or for a finite number of gestures (▾), specified by generator below
  • if pass to neighbor is checked, when this player is done, randomly select an adjacent player (i.e. in the same track or rack) and play it

Viewer

The Viewer displays generated gestures, one at a time, as Loom only generates one gesture at a time.

Loop

When Loop is enabled, previously generated gestures are repeated, and highlighted in the loop display as they are being output.

  • the number of gestures may be adjusted by the generator

Rhythm

Rhythm determines the rhythmic structure of each new gesture.

  • the topmost generator is the beat according to Live
    e.g. 1 = one beat = one quarter note = one crotchet
  • the second generator multiplies or divides that beat, based on the selection of the × or ÷ operator
    e.g. multiplying by 3 yields "dotted" notes, dividing by 3 yields "triplets"
  • the × • generator determines the number of notes
  • the × ◦ generator determines the number of rests
  • if post is selected, rests will occur after the notes, not before

Accent

The Accent generator determines where the "accent" (highest velocity) is placed in the gesture—earlier or later.
Actual velocity values are hard-coded to 128 (accented) versus 64 (unaccented).

Pitch

The Pitch region sets the pitch of each note in each gesture.

  • if MIDI in is checked, notes may be selected by MIDI in this track
  • if toggle is additionally checked, notes will be alternately selected or unselected based on MIDI input
  • pitch range is determined by the three-octave span of the generator

General

  • If there are multiple players in a same track, their MIDI events will ALL be output from the LAST player. MIDI is passed through each, and can be accessed in any player. (But if Pitch MIDI in is used, MIDI thru will be blocked at that player.)
  • When a gesture parameter is edited, the viewer will display a preview of a possible resulting gesture (assuming deviation of all controls is zero).
  • Players may be nested in any number of racks for organization or storage purposes.
